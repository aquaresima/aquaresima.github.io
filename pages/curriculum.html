Doctoral Research

“A bridge not too far. Neurobiologically constrained model of spoken word recognition."

My research examined the role of dendritic computations in processing word memories with phonological overlap. The work combines insights from computational neuroscience with experimental evidence in word recognition. The result is a computational model that proposes a mapping hypothesis between neurobiological and psychological processes.

Research and Teaching Experience

2023 – 2024 FT Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics, Nijmegen PostDoc position at NBL Department After the conclusion of my Ph.D. dissertation, I continued working on the spiking neural network simulator developed for the project. The goal is to have a flexible tool for neurobiologically detailed simulations.

2019 – 2023 FT Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics, Nijmegen Ph.D. Candidate I achieved my Ph.D. degree in Psychology of Language. Part of the work involved developing a Julia library for integrating and analyzing simulations of spiking neural networks with dendrites. I also gained experience in presenting my work to large audiences.

2021 – 2023 FT Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics, Nijmegen Thesis supervision During my Ph.D. I co-supervised several bachelor theses on the encoding of acoustic information in spiking neural networks.

2014 – 2019 PT The Science Zone – Elementary schools, Rome Science teacher in elementary schools I was an active member of a science outreach association that used the Inquiry-Based Scientific Education method to teach young students Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
